Helsinki

In 1952, at the Helsinki Olympic games, Emil Zatopek, running for Czechoslovakia, won the 5,000m, 10,000m and Marathon, breaking the Olympic record in each event. Zatopek’s amazing triple has never been matched.

Tokyo

As a virtual unknown, American Billy Mills took the 10,000m by storm at the Tokyo Olympics, running a world record time and becoming the only person from the Western Hemisphere to win the event at the Olympics.

Rome

At the 1960 Olympics, Ethiopian Abebe Bikila ran the Streets of Rome barefoot in the marathon, and won!

LA

At the 1984 Olympics in LA, the women’s marathon was run for the first time, and won by American marathon legend Joan Beniot, who holds womens marathon records to this day!

London

London 1908 was another first, as Dorando Pietri crossed the line first in the first ever Olympic marathon. However, the last 400 metres took him over 10 minutes as he collapsed on a number of occasions and had to be helped over the line, an action which ultimately got him disqualified.
