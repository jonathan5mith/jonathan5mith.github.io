How Kick Works

Kicks and Events

What's a Kick?

A kick is a weekly 5k online race. Each week, between Monday and Sunday you create or join up to 3 kicks, run your race and submit your time to Kick.

The twist is, Kick’s not just about pure speed, you're awarded a position, and points based on how well you’ve done compared to your recent performance.

What's an Event?

Events are monthly online races over different distances. Events follow a more traditional race format, but there are little challenges every month to keep you on your toes.


You can find out about events on the latest events page and Kicks are explained more below.

Getting started

Before you can start creating Kicks and challenging your friends, you need to sign up using your twitter handle and send us your best 5k time from the last 6 months. You don’t need to send this immediately, but you’ll need to before you can kick.

As soon as you’ve signed up you’ll have a profile, and Kick will send you a unique ID (your Kick ID) which you’ll use to create Kicks and challenge others.

Create and Enter Kicks

When you’ve signed up and submitted your first time, you’ll be ready to create your own Kick, or enter Kicks that others have made. Remember, you’ll use your Kick ID to do both.

When you’ve created a Kick, you’ll be able to share it with your twitter followers (could be one, could be hundreds) and they’ll be able to enter your race.

You can also choose for your Kick to be added to the explore page, where anyone else can see it.

Results and Vests

When Monday comes around, it’s results day. Your profile will be updated with your points, times and any vests you’ve won, and we’ll tweet you your results.

If you win your Kick you’ll always get 10 points, and points for everyone else depends on the numbers who took part. You’ll always get at least 1 point though!

FAQs

Who are you exactly? Hi, I’m Jon. I love running and making ideas happen, so I made Kick. You can find out more here.

Do I have to be on Twitter? Yes, for the moment you do as I’m trying to keep things simple to start with, and Twitter is a good way to manage users and for people to share and challenge (there’s a great running community on twitter). You could always just sign up so you can Kick (it doesn’t mean you have to follow Justin Bieber).

I’m interested in cheating, is Kick for me? No. Obviously, being virtual, Kick is open to abuse, but Kick’s about having fun and challenging yourself to do better. You have to be a special kind of loser to cheat for online badges.
